What is a cage?

The cages aren't flaws.
They're adaptations.

Every cage started as a survival strategy. You learned — through praise, punishment, and the subtle grammar of belonging — that a certain version of yourself was acceptable. You built that version carefully. You got very good at it.

The problem is you're still living there. And the woman inside the cage has been waiting a long time to be found.

About

Jordan Scotti, PhD

Licensed Psychologist · Yoga Teacher · Writer

I spent years sitting across from women who were exhausted, high-functioning, and completely disconnected from themselves. I also spent years being one of them. The cage framework came out of that — out of clinical work, out of yoga training, out of Jungian psychology, and out of the particular kind of reckoning that happens in midlife when the strategies stop working.

The Deep End is where I write about what I've learned. Not as advice. As someone who is still in it.
